Executive Summary

This report presents the findings of the initial Day 1 field observation conducted at Justin Property Group, Mokopane, as part of the XBIT Work Integrated Learning engagement. The observation encompassed stakeholder identification, role and responsibility mapping, and a preliminary operational situational analysis.

Six internal stakeholders were identified across executive, management, sales, and administrative functions. Observations revealed a capable and professionally engaged team operating with notable gaps in digital workflow automation, internal communication infrastructure, and management reporting tooling. The most critical finding — a manual, 2-hour process for bulk client communication — is representative of a broader pattern of underutilised technology that, if addressed, could deliver significant competitive advantage in a market the principal himself characterises as increasingly saturated.

Four priority recommendations are proposed for further investigation and solution design in subsequent WIL phases, ranging from immediate automation wins to medium-term CRM and reporting implementation.

Client communications are executed through a fully manual process: client data must be manually exported from spreadsheets, reformatted, and imported into a bulk SMS platform. → Impact: Lost leads, inconsistent follow-ups, institutional knowledge risk when staff leave.

Principal Task Overload — Poor Delegation
The principal is personally responsible for property photography using a personal iPhone 16, alongside executive-level responsibilities including regulatory compliance, digital marketing, and business development. This creates a strategic bottleneck — high-value executive attention is consumed by tasks that could be delegated or automated.
→ Impact: Reduced strategic capacity, delayed listings, executive burnout risk.
🟠 High
No Management Reporting Layer
PayProp manages transactional data effectively, but there is no reporting or analytics layer (e.g., Power BI, Xero) to translate operational data into decision-making insights. Management cannot easily view performance trends, revenue forecasts, agent productivity metrics, or portfolio health indicators without manual report compilation.
→ Impact: Reactive rather than data-driven management. Competitive disadvantage in a saturating market.
🟠 High
Uncontrolled WhatsApp Communication
Client communication via WhatsApp is the regional standard and cannot be eliminated. However, shared account access between administration and reception creates consistency risk — different staff members may respond differently to the same client enquiry. No automated response system exists for after-hours or peak-volume periods.
→ Impact: Communication inconsistency, missed enquiries during peak periods, no audit trail.
🔵 Notable
Third-Party Vendor Quality Inconsistency
Maintenance and cleaning crews engaged for rental property management demonstrate inconsistent service quality. While ingoing and outgoing inspection protocols exist as a mitigation measure, service delivery failures continue to reach clients, affecting tenant satisfaction and rental renewal rates.
→ Impact: Client dissatisfaction, reputational risk, increased receptionist workload for complaint resolution.
🔵 Notable
No Formalised Internal Communication System
Information flow between departments relies primarily on physical proximity — staff must be present to receive updates. There is no internal messaging system, task management tool, or shared digital workspace. This creates inefficiencies when staff are out of office, on site visits, or working with clients remotely.
→ Impact: Information silos, duplicated work, dependency on physical presence for operations.

Priority Recommendations

The following four recommendations are proposed for investigation and implementation during the subsequent WIL phases. They are ordered by business impact and implementation urgency.

1
Automated Client Communication System (CRM + Bulk SMS Integration)
Replace the manual Excel → CSV → SMS App workflow with an integrated client database and one-click campaign tool. Client data maintained in a live system, campaigns launched in under 5 minutes, delivery reports generated automatically. Directly eliminates the 2-hour process observed on Day 1 and enables more frequent, consistent client engagement.
⚡ Quick Win 🛠 Technical 📋 Process
2
Website Enhancement — Lead Capture CRM Integration
Justin Property Group already operates a branded website at justinproperty.co.za — a significant asset many competitors lack. The opportunity is to evolve this from a static listing display into an active lead generation engine: enquiry forms that auto-populate a CRM pipeline, client segmentation, and automated follow-up triggers. Agents would receive instant notifications when a buyer engages with a listing online.
📅 Medium Term 🛠 Technical
3
Management Reporting Dashboard (PayProp Data Layer)
Build a reporting interface that aggregates PayProp data into visual dashboards — rental income trends, maintenance ticket resolution times, lease renewal rates, and agent performance metrics. Enables data-driven management decisions without manual report compilation. Addresses the absence of Power BI or equivalent tooling currently identified.
📅 Medium Term 🛠 Technical 📋 Process
4
Task Delegation Framework & Photography Workflow
Develop a structured delegation protocol to offload non-executive tasks from the principal. Immediate recommendation: formalise the property photography workflow — engage a consistent freelance photographer or train an existing staff member with a dedicated camera. The principal's time is the organisation's most valuable and currently most underprotected resource.

II. Deeds Office — Four-Stage Examination Process
Once all legal and financial prerequisites are met, attorneys arrange simultaneous lodgment. The Deeds Office processes all related transactions as a single batch.
1
Stage 1
Lodgment & Batching
Deeds submitted to the Deeds Office. Transfer, Cancellation, and Registration transactions are linked and processed as a single batch to ensure legal continuity. Batch integrity is mandatory — no partial processing.
2
Stage 2
Level 1 & Data Verification
Preliminary administrative checks performed. Comprehensive data searches conducted to identify any interdicts against parties or the property. Any interdict at this stage stops the entire batch.
3
Stage 3
Level 2 & 3 Senior Review
Detailed scrutiny by senior examiners to ensure full compliance with the Deeds Registries Act. This is the most rigorous examination stage — any non-compliance results in a requisition note requiring attorney correction.
4
Stage 4
Prep Phase & Registration
Attorneys notified to perform final financial and conditional audits. All linked parties must issue "pro-reg" (proceed to registration) instructions. Registrar of Deeds formally executes and signs. 5-day compliance window — failure to register within 5 working days causes automatic batch rejection.
⚠️
CRITICAL COMPLIANCE WINDOW
Upon receiving "prep" notification, attorneys must register within five working days. Failure to meet this deadline results in automatic rejection of the entire batch — requiring the entire lodgment process to restart from Stage 1. This is the highest-risk point in the conveyancing lifecycle and a prime candidate for automated deadline tracking within the proposed CRM system.
💡 WIL Prototype Implication — Transaction Tracker

The conveyancing lifecycle presents a clear opportunity for a Deal Transaction Tracker module within the proposed platform. Each active property deal could be tracked through all three workstreams and the four Deeds Office stages, with automated status updates, deadline alerts (especially the critical 5-day prep window), and document checklist visibility for the Branch Manager and Principal.
